> ### Visualizing Hierarchy
> 
> In your role as a Regional Director, you already understand hierarchy (Company > Department > Team). HTML is the same. Think of it like a newspaper or a professional project proposal:
> 
> * **`<h1>`**: The Title (Only one per page).
> * **`<h2>`**: The Major Sections (e.g., "Market Analysis," "Budget").
> * **`<h3>` to `<h6>**`: Sub-points within those sections.
> * **`<p>`**: The actual conversation or details.
> 
> ### Best Practices for Your Success
> 
> Since you mentioned needing to stand out and handle "entitled" teams with clarity, treat your code like your project management style: **Clean, structured, and defensive.**
> 
> | Tag | Level | Real-world Analogy |
> | --- | --- | --- |
> | `<h1>` | Highest | The Project Name |
> | `<h2>` | High | Project Phases (Design, Build) |
> | `<h3>` | Medium | Tasks within Phases |
> | `<p>` | Neutral | Meeting Notes / Descriptions |
> 
> **Pro-Tip:** Never use an `<h3>` just because you want the text to look "bold" or "smaller." Use CSS for styling. Use HTML tags only to define the **importance** and **structure** of the content. This "Semantic" approach makes your websites professional and accessible.
